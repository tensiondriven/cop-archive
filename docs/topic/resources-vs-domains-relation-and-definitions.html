    2. Andrei Miron 2017-04-12 (04/12/2017)

      Hi [@mention:553141978014385339] in the Constitution article 1.1(b) the definition of the Domain is about "things". If you replace "things" with "resource" then you have the difference. 

      The Domain is the "fence" around any resource of the Organization which is exclusively control by a Role or a Circle. As long as the control is not delegated then is a "Resource" which can be impacted or controlled by anyone as long as is not forbidden by the Constitution or a Policy.

    3. Krzysztof Holeczko 2017-04-13 (04/13/2017)

      Thanks to both of you :-) Just to check I'll try to answer the original question myself. Please confirm/change/add sth.

      1. What is the relation between resources and domains? (are the same?, are different and if so, how to clearly differentiate them? Maybe resources>domains or maybe otherwise?)

      There is no logic relation (=, <, > etc.) between RESOUCES and DOMAINS, as they belong to different categories.
      RESOURCES=THINGS (it can be a car, computer, www website, database, file, production machine etc.)
      DOMAIN=PROPERTY or FENCE (such fence can be built around barely anything, including RESOURCES, but also other intangible beings like for example "processes" or  "communication with a specific client")
