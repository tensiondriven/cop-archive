    2. Bernard Marie Chiquet 2018-08-22 (08/22/2018)

      [@mention:568484858377802720] As stated in constitution section 2.2.3 : "However, a Circle may remove any accountability, or functions of its Lead Link Role, either by placing them on another Role within the Circle, or by defining an alternate means of enacting them."

      As an example, and if required by tension or reality, you may create a Policy that states something like "Instead of Lead Link authority, the roles of the circle will be affected using the integrative election process.". Note, I'm not saying you should do that, I'm just saying technically you may do that in alignment with the Constitution.

    3. Bernard Marie Chiquet 2018-08-21 (08/21/2018)

      [@mention:568484858377802720] You're right, Holacracy is about a purpose driven organization. Notice that Lead Link is a Role that covers functions required for leading a Circle. Per default in Constitution 4.1, one person fills such Role, but you may change that by distributing such functions on different Roles or processes. For instance, you could decide (by adding a Policy) to elect all the Roles within a Circle, if this was the way used before Holacracy adoption.
